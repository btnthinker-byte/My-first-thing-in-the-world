About Lions

Lions (Panthera leo) are the only truly social cats, living in family groups called prides, primarily in African savannas and a small population in India's Gir Forest.
Known as apex predators, lionesses do most hunting for prey like zebra and wildebeest, while males defend territory.
They are highly territorial, and their roar can be heard up to 8 km away.

    Key Characteristics and Behavior
  1. Physical Appearance: Males are larger, weighing over 550 lbs, and possess a distinct mane that signifies health and fighting ability. Females (lionesses) lack manes and are the primary hunters.
  2. Social Structure: Lions are the only cats that live in groups called prides, typically consisting of related females, their cubs, and 1–2 dominant males.
  3. Hunting and Diet: As carnivores, they hunt large ungulates like wildebeest, zebras, and buffalo. Lionesses use teamwork to hunt, often at night, though they may scavenge for half their food.
  4. Activity: They are notoriously lazy, sleeping or resting for up to 20 hours a day to conserve energy.
  5. Life Cycle: Cubs are born in litters of 1–6 and are cared for communally by females in the pride.
